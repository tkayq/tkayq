## About

I develop electronic structure methods and the performance-oriented scientific software that makes them practical on modern hardware. My work spans method development in variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and coupled cluster theory for excited states, and the C++/HPC engineering needed to bring these methods to larger systems. I'm especially interested in the space between wavefunction-based accuracy and molecular-dynamics-scale efficiency — e.g. machine-learned potentials and scalable post-Hartree–Fock models.

## Research Interests

- ⚛️ **High-accuracy electronic structure theory** — Aufbau-suppressed coupled cluster for charge-transfer excited states, achieving sub-0.2 eV accuracy where standard linear-response methods struggle
- 🎲 **Variational Monte Carlo method development** — variance-reduction and memory-efficient linear-method optimization for large parameter sets (e.g. LCAO coefficients), improving stability without sacrificing accuracy
- 🧮 **Local, linear-scaling electronic structure** — a performance-focused C++ implementation of local correlation methods, profiled and optimized on Intel architectures (MKL, OpenMP, VTune)
- 🌉 **Bridging QM accuracy and MD scale** — scalable post-Hartree–Fock models and machine-learned potentials for large, complex systems
- 🖥️ **HPC workflow design** — batched submission and processing pipelines for thousands of quantum chemistry calculations across SLURM clusters
